How the story began.

Runescape is a very popular MMORPG, by Jagex Ltd. The game was designed by Andrew Gower, and released on january 4th 2001. The game was developed in Java at first, but later got switched to their own programming language called Runescript. This allowed for faster updates.


The game was first released as 'DeviousMUD', also made by Andrew Gower in 1998. The game was in beta at the time, and development continued in 1999. During this period, the game was playable for one week. Approximately 100 players played during this period, before it went offline again after 8 days.

Runescape Classic, and the surge in population

In 2001, DeviousMUD was replaced by Runescape, it was the first playable version of the game, and was totally free. The game was free at first, until it became payment-based in 2002. In august of 2005, Runescape Classic was available only to paying players, that played in the first 6 months of their membership.


In 2004, the game had 1 million active players, and in 2007, the game had 8 million active players. In 2008, the game had 10 million active players, and in 2010, the game had 12 million active players. In 2012, the game had 10 million active players, and in 2013, the game had 200 million active players. In 2013, Oldschool runescape was launched. It was released from a build from 2007, and has been receiving content updates ever since.
